Plain Sawn

The most common and cost-effective method. The log is cut tangentially to the growth rings.

Pros

  • High yield (least waste)
  • Fast and cheap to produce
  • Beautiful "cathedral" grain pattern

Cons

  • Less stable (prone to cupping/twisting)
  • Expands/contracts more with moisture
